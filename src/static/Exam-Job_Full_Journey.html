Most candidates clear NQT but fail the technical interview due to weak CS fundamentals and no projects to talk about. Both must be prepared together — not sequentially.

This stage is already covered in detail in your 8-week roadmap. This is a quick reference summary.

NQT sections and marks weightage
1Verbal Ability — RC, error spotting, synonyms, para jumbles. ~24 questions, 20 mins
2Reasoning Ability — series, blood relations, seating, puzzles. ~30 questions, 50 mins
3Numerical Ability — arithmetic, DI, percentages. ~26 questions, 40 mins
4Programming Logic — output prediction, OOP MCQs, complexity. ~10 questions, 15 mins
5Coding — 2 problems, any language. Easy to Medium difficulty. 45 mins
Passing criteria
TCS has sectional cutoffs — you must clear every section individually, not just total score. Scoring high in Coding but failing Aptitude = rejection. Aim for 70%+ in each section.
Day before exam checklist
Hall ticket downloaded and printed
Government ID ready (Aadhar / PAN)
Exam center location confirmed
Woke up early — at center 30 mins before
No new topics — only light revision
Sleep 7+ hours — performance depends on it

Negative marking exists in NQT. If you're not sure, skip — don't guess randomly. Attempt questions you know first, then return to uncertain ones.

What the interviewer is checking
→Do you understand the basics or just memorized them?
→Can you explain clearly without getting confused?
→Do you have any real project experience?
→Can you write basic code on paper/screen?

OS
Process/Thread, Deadlock, Scheduling, Paging, Virtual Memory, Semaphore

OSI 7 layers, TCP vs UDP, HTTP/HTTPS, DNS, 3-way handshake

4 pillars, Overloading vs Overriding, Abstract class vs Interface, Constructors
DBMS
Normalization, ACID, Keys, Joins, DELETE vs TRUNCATE vs DROP
Project questions — always asked
Tell me about your project
Prepare a 60-second pitch: what it does, tech stack, your role, one challenge you solved
What was the most difficult part?
Never say "nothing was difficult" — pick one real problem and how you solved it
Why did you choose this technology?
Have a reason ready — even "it's the industry standard for this use case" is fine
How would you improve this project?
Shows growth mindset — prepare 2–3 improvements you haven't built yet
Coding on paper / screen
TCS technical interviews sometimes ask you to write 5–15 lines of code. Common: reverse a string, check palindrome, find factorial, basic sorting. Practice writing clean code without IDE help.

If you don't know an answer, say "I'm not fully sure, but my understanding is..." — honesty + logical thinking impresses interviewers far more than bluffing.

Not all TCS interviews include the Managerial Round. If you get it, it means you're doing well — they're checking leadership and attitude fit.

What MR actually tests
→Can you handle pressure without breaking?
→Are you a team player or a lone wolf?
→Do you communicate clearly and confidently?
→Are you adaptable to different roles and locations?
Common MR questions + strategy
Tell me about yourself
60 seconds: name → degree → skills → projects → why TCS. Practice this until natural.
Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
Be ambitious but grounded: "I want to grow into a technical lead role, contributing to large-scale projects."
Tell me about a time you worked in a team conflict
Use STAR method — Situation, Task, Action, Result. Have one real story ready.
Why TCS?
Research TCS's scale, training programs (TCS iON, Ignite), and global exposure. Be specific — not "it's a good company."
Are you open to relocation?
Always say yes in MR — negotiation comes later. Saying no here is a red flag.
What is your biggest weakness?
Pick a real weakness that you're actively improving. Never say "I work too hard."

Speak slowly and confidently. Most MR rejections happen due to low confidence, not wrong answers. Practice your answers out loud — not in your head.

HR round purpose
HR is not trying to fail you — at this stage they want to offer you the job. They're checking: background consistency, salary expectations, joining timeline, and attitude fit. Stay calm and honest.
Common HR questions
What is your expected salary?
For freshers at TCS, packages are mostly fixed. Just say "I'm flexible with the standard fresher package and focused on the opportunity." Don't negotiate hard here.
Do you have any backlogs?
Be truthful. TCS has a strict no-active-backlog policy at joining time. Historical backlogs are usually okay if cleared.
When can you join?
After exams / after graduation — be specific. Don't say "anytime" as it sounds unplanned.
Do you have any other offers?
You can say yes honestly — it shows you're a strong candidate. Don't lie either way.
Why should we hire you?
Combine your technical skills + attitude: "I have a strong foundation in [skills], I learn quickly, and I'm committed to delivering quality work."

Resume — non-negotiable rules for TCS
1One page only — TCS recruiters spend 30 seconds on fresher resumes
2No photo, no date of birth, no gender — modern standard
3Reverse chronological order — latest first
4Use action verbs — Built, Designed, Implemented, Optimized
5No spelling mistakes — use Grammarly to check
6PDF format only — never Word doc
Sections in order
→Header — Name, phone, email, LinkedIn, GitHub
→Education — Degree, college, CGPA, year. 10th + 12th below.
→Skills — Languages, frameworks, tools, databases
→Projects — 2–3 projects with tech stack + 2 bullet points each
→Certifications — TCS iON, NPTEL, Coursera (relevant only)
→Achievements — Hackathons, competitions, college events
LinkedIn — must have before interview

TCS HR Googles your name before the interview. A good LinkedIn profile with projects can genuinely make them more excited to meet you.

How to present your project in interview
1What it does — one sentence, non-technical
2Tech stack — what and why each technology
3Your role — what you personally built
4Challenge solved — one specific problem you faced and fixed
5Future scope — what you'd add next

Even a simple project explained with full depth impresses more than a complex project you can't explain. Know your project inside out.

After HR — what happens next
1Result declared via email + TCS NextStep portal — check daily
2Offer letter released on NextStep — download and read carefully
3iBegin registration — mandatory for joining formalities
4Document upload on portal — all marksheets, ID proofs
5Medical fitness test — arranged by TCS
6Joining date assigned — typically 3–6 months after offer
7Initial Training — ILP (Initial Learning Program) at TCS facility

During ILP Training — stay sharp
→ILP covers Java basics, SQL, soft skills, TCS processes — attend seriously
→ILP has tests — poor performance can affect your role assignment
→Network with batchmates — your first professional connections
→Keep learning MERN and DSA — role assignment is 6–12 months out

Many TCS freshers get a generic role but move to better projects within 1–2 years by performing well in ILP and getting internal certifications. The job is the beginning, not the destination.
