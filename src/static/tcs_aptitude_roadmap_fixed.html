Shortcuts you must memorise
Percentage — fast calculation
To find X% of Y → find 10% first, then scale. Example: 35% of 240 → 10%=24, 30%=72, 5%=12 → 35%=84. Never multiply directly.
Profit & Loss — shortcut
If CP=100, SP=120 → profit 20%. Discount is always on MP. Successive discounts of a% and b% = a+b−(ab/100). Memorise this formula.
Time & Work — unitary method
If A does work in 'a' days and B in 'b' days → together = ab/(a+b) days. Same logic applies directly to Pipes & Cisterns.
Speed Distance Time — relative speed
Same direction: speeds subtract. Opposite direction: speeds add. For trains crossing: distance = sum of lengths. Boats: upstream = B−S, downstream = B+S.
HCF & LCM — product rule
HCF × LCM = Product of two numbers. Always. Use this to cross-check your answer or find one when the other is given.
Compound Interest — quick 2-year trick
CI for 2 years = SI for 2 years + SI on first year's interest. Avoids the long formula completely for 2-year questions.
Clocks — angle formula
Angle between hands at H hours M minutes = |30H − 5.5M|. If answer > 180, subtract from 360. One formula for every clock question.
Ratio & Proportion — cross multiply
If a:b = c:d then ad = bc. For compound ratios: multiply all numerators together and all denominators together.

Don't memorise shortcuts without solving questions immediately after. Learn the shortcut → solve 10 questions using it → it sticks permanently.

Error log — most powerful habit
Every wrong question → write topic, why you went wrong, correct approach. Review every Sunday. After 3 weeks you'll see clear patterns — those are your weak spots to target in mock tests.
